# Theory Notes

## Greimasian Semiotics

A.J. Greimas's actantial model describes narrative as a structure of six actantial roles:

- **Subject** — the hero or protagonist pursuing an Object.
- **Object** — the goal, value, or desired entity.
- **Sender** — the force that instigates the action.
- **Receiver** — the beneficiary of the action.
- **Helper** — forces that aid the Subject.
- **Opponent** — forces that hinder the Subject.

The narrative program (NP) formalizes the transformation from a state of lack to a state of fulfilled junction.

## Proppian Morphology

Vladimir Propp identified 31 narrative functions in Russian folktales. These functions follow a fixed sequence and can be mapped onto actantial roles. The engine uses a subset of Propp's functions as a vocabulary for story beats.

## Fabula vs Sujet

- **Fabula**: the raw chronological sequence of events in causal-logical order.
- **Sujet**: the order and manner in which events are presented to the audience.

The engine generates fabula first, then applies sujet transformations (flashback, in medias res, etc.) at the scene level.
