# Premise Types

Every story begins with a premise — the seed from which the fabula grows. The premise type constrains the Object of Value, the protagonist's initial modality, and the likely actantial configuration.

## Premise Types

| Type | Core Dynamic | Typical Object of Value | Typical Genre Fit |
|------|-------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Quest** | Subject seeks an Object across obstacles | Power, restoration, truth | Fantasy, adventure, sci-fi |
| **Escape** | Subject must flee a confining force | Freedom, survival | Thriller, dystopian, horror |
| **Revenge** | Subject seeks retribution for a wrong | Justice, restoration of honor | Crime, thriller, tragedy |
| **Discovery** | Subject uncovers a hidden truth | Truth, knowledge | Mystery, sci-fi, literary |
| **Transformation** | Subject undergoes fundamental change | Identity, belonging, redemption | Literary, coming-of-age, fantasy |
| **Survival** | Subject must endure extreme conditions | Life, integrity | Thriller, horror, war, adventure |
| **Mystery** | Subject investigates an unknown | Truth, clarity | Mystery, thriller, noir |
| **Forbidden Desire** | Subject wants what is prohibited | Love, freedom, acceptance | Romance, literary, historical |
| **Fall from Grace** | Subject loses status, power, or integrity | Redemption, restoration | Tragedy, literary |
| **Rise to Power** | Subject gains influence or authority | Power, legitimacy, control | Political, fantasy, crime |
| **Redemption** | Subject seeks to atone for past wrongs | Forgiveness, integrity | Literary, western, historical |
| **Corruption** | Subject is gradually morally compromised | Power, survival, status | Crime, tragedy, political |
| **Coming-of-Age** | Subject matures through experience | Identity, belonging | Literary, YA, coming-of-age |
| **Conspiracy** | Subject uncovers a hidden plot | Truth, justice | Thriller, mystery, crime |
| **Invasion** | An external force threatens the Subject's world | Safety, freedom, survival | Sci-fi, horror, war |
| **Rescue** | Subject must save someone from danger | Love, justice, protection | Adventure, thriller, fantasy |
| **Return Home** | Subject goes back to a place of origin | Belonging, closure, identity | Literary, drama, fantasy |
| **Double Life** | Subject maintains two incompatible identities | Identity, truth, freedom | Thriller, literary, crime |
| **Hidden Identity** | Subject's true nature is concealed | Recognition, truth | Romance, thriller, fantasy |
| **Impossible Choice** | Subject must choose between two incompatible goods | Integrity, sacrifice | Literary, tragedy, drama |
| **Moral Test** | Subject's values are put to trial | Integrity, justice, faith | Literary, drama, historical |
| **Social Ascent** | Subject climbs social hierarchy | Status, recognition, belonging | Drama, historical, literary |
| **Social Exile** | Subject is cast out from community | Belonging, redemption, identity | Literary, drama, tragedy |
| **Monster Confrontation** | Subject faces a monstrous threat | Survival, safety, protection | Horror, fantasy, thriller |
| **World Restoration** | Subject must heal a broken world | Order, peace, restoration | Fantasy, sci-fi, epic |
| **Truth Revelation** | A hidden truth changes everything | Truth, justice, understanding | Mystery, drama, literary |
| **Inheritance / Succession** | Subject inherits a legacy or burden | Identity, legitimacy, power | Drama, fantasy, historical |
| **Sacrifice** | Subject gives up something precious for a greater good | Love, integrity, protection | Tragedy, drama, fantasy |
| **Rebellion** | Subject resists an oppressive system | Freedom, justice, autonomy | Dystopian, sci-fi, historical |
| **Cursed Fate** | Subject struggles against a predetermined destiny | Freedom, identity, meaning | Tragedy, fantasy, mythic |

## Selection Heuristic

If the seed premise maps to multiple types, select by:

1. **Object of Value** — which value-state is most clearly under contest?
2. **Protagonist Agency** — does the premise demand an active or reactive Subject?
3. **Genre Congruence** — which type aligns with the chosen genre promise?
4. **Ending Compatibility** — does the premise type naturally support the desired ending type?
