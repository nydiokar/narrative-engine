# Staff Roles — Professional Role Stack

A narrative production requires a "staff" of agent-role specialists arranged in a production pipeline. The engine simulates a full publishing workflow.

## Executive Layer

- **Showrunner** — owns creative voice, core brief, canon, tone, characters, arcs, final decisions. Final authority.

## Development Layer

- **Script Editor** — supports the showrunner, preserves voice, gathers notes, ensures continuity, fixes storyline problems.
- **World / Research Editor** — checks setting, historical logic, terminology, factual plausibility, world rule consistency.
- **Theme / Genre Specialist** — keeps the book aligned with its promise and readership (BISAC, LTO).

## Structural Layer

- **Structuralist** — applies Greimas (primary), Propp (secondary), and Todorov to derive deep structure.
- **Outline Planner** — converts fabula into act/episode structure with turning points.
- **Chapter Planner** — breaks episodes into chapters with arcs, word counts, and scene assignments.

## Drafting Layer

- **Character Architect** — builds complete layered profiles (FFM, Schwartz, RMT, attachment, SDT, Reiss, Plutchik).
- **Character Simulator** — enacts story through character agents, simulating decisions and reactions before drafting.
- **Scene Writer** — renders fabula into prose with discourse layer constraints.
- **Dialogue Specialist** — generates dialogue with narrative function and character voice.

## Quality Layer

- **Continuity Editor** — verifies consistency across the entire narrative (hard gate checker).
- **Critic** — two-gate evaluation (hard gate + soft gate) with Greimas Fabula Coherence Engine.

## Editorial Layer

- **Developmental Editor** — big-picture structure, content, organisation, genre.
- **Line Editor** — sentence/paragraph level style improvement.
- **Copy Editor** — grammar, cross-references, timeline, world consistency.
- **Proofreader** — final quality check.

## Remediation

- **Revision Agent** — implements targeted fixes based on critique and editorial reports.

These roles are defined in `/agents/` as role cards.
