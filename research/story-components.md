# Story Components

A narrative is composed of the following irreducible components:

1. **Actants** — the deep roles that drive action (Subject, Object, Sender, Receiver, Helper, Opponent).
2. **Events** — discrete changes of state, each governed by a narrative program.
3. **Objects of Value** — the entities, states, or qualities that actants seek or possess.
4. **Modalities** — the qualifications of action (wanting, knowing, being-able, having-to).
5. **Temporal Relations** — ordering, duration, frequency of events.
6. **Causal Relations** — the logical connective tissue between events.

These components are assembled into a fabula, which is then rendered as a sujet.
