# Tone, Mood, and Style Maps

The discourse layer (sujet) governs how the fabula is presented. Tone, mood, and prose style are the primary instruments of discourse.

## Tone Spectrums

Tone is the narrator's attitude toward the material. It operates on spectrums:

| Spectrum | Pole A | Pole B |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Earnest ↔ Ironic | Sincere, direct | Detached, knowing |
| Dark ↔ Light | Grim, heavy | Bright, buoyant |
| Intimate ↔ Epic | Personal, close | Vast, sweeping |
| Cold ↔ Warm | Distant, clinical | Emotional, generous |
| Sparse ↔ Lush | Minimal, economic | Ornate, textured |
| Realist ↔ Mythic | Grounded, plausible | Symbolic, archetypal |
| Brutal ↔ Tender | Harsh, unsparing | Gentle, merciful |
| Detached ↔ Emotional | Observational | Immersive, feeling |
| Satirical ↔ Reverent | Mocking, critical | Respectful, awed |
| Bleak ↔ Hopeful | Pessimistic, grim | Optimistic, forward-looking |
| Dreamlike ↔ Concrete | Surreal, fluid | Specific, physical |
| Elegant ↔ Raw | Polished, refined | Rough, unvarnished |
| Fast ↔ Meditative | Propulsive, urgent | Slow, reflective |

## Prose Style Types

| Style | Characteristics | Best Fit For |
|-------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Minimalist** | Short sentences, simple diction, understatement | Thriller, realism, emotional restraint |
| **Lyrical** | Rhythm, metaphor, sensory richness | Literary, mythic, grief, memory, longing |
| **Cinematic** | Visual, scene-oriented, cuts between action | Action, adventure, thriller, fantasy |
| **Mythic** | Elevated, formulaic, archetypal language | Fantasy, tragedy, epic |
| **Noir** | Hardboiled, cynical, urban, moral ambiguity | Crime, mystery, corruption, fatalism |
| **Satirical** | Exaggerated, ironic, critical | Social critique, absurd institutions |
| **Epistolary** | Letters, documents, diary entries | Historical, mystery, literary |
| **Stream-of-Consciousness** | Interior, associative, unstructured | Literary, psychological |
| **Oral Storytelling** | Direct address, repetition, folktale rhythm | Fantasy, mythic, YA |
| **Fragmentary** | Broken syntax, gaps, ellipses | Literary, experimental, trauma |
| **Dense Literary** | Complex sentences, rich vocabulary, ambiguity | Literary fiction |
| **Transparent Commercial** | Invisible style, clear prose, fast reading | Genre fiction, thriller, romance |
| **Gothic** | Ornate, atmospheric, decay, dread | Horror, inheritance, hidden sin |
| **Baroque** | Highly ornamental, elaborate, extravagant | Fantasy, historical, literary |
| **Spare Brutalist** | Blunt, repetitive, forceful | Crime, war, survival |
| **Poetic Realist** | Lyrical grounded in physical specificity | Literary, historical |

## Style Compatibility Matrix

| Style | Works With | Conflicts With |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Minimalist | Thriller, noir, war | Epic fantasy, romance |
| Lyrical | Literary, mythic, grief | Hardboiled crime, thriller |
| Cinematic | Action, adventure, fantasy | Stream-of-consciousness |
| Gothic | Horror, historical, romance | Minimalist, commercial |
| Satirical | Comedy, social critique | Earnest epic, romance |
| Mythic | Fantasy, tragedy | Realist, literary minimalism |
| Transparent Commercial | Any genre fiction | Literary experimental |

## Mood Palette

Mood is the emotional atmosphere of a scene. It is set by the intersection of:
- Scene type (a Battle scene defaults to tense/dread)
- Emotional tone of the scene's dominant actant
- Physical setting (weather, light, sound, space)
- Stakes intensity

Mood should shift across a scene to avoid flatness. Standard mood trajectory: establish → build → peak → release.
