# Quality Principles

The Critic agent evaluates narratives against these dimensions, powered by the Greimas Fabula Coherence Engine.

## Greimas Fabula Coherence Engine — Diagnostic Checks

The following 8 checks are the primary anti-filler and coherence mechanism of the engine:

1. **Detect scenes that do not transform state** — Every scene must change at least one parameter (junction, modality, or value-object definition). If no transformation occurred, the scene is filler.

2. **Detect conflicts without clear value-object** — A conflict where the underlying value-state is undefined or unmotivated is false drama.

3. **Detect protagonists without object-desire** — A Subject who does not actively desire an Object of value is a passive protagonist. The engine flags missing wanting-modality.

4. **Detect antagonists who merely "oppose" without competing value-logic** — An Opponent must have their own relationship to the value-object. If they only obstruct without their own stake, the conflict is shallow.

5. **Detect missing qualification before performance** — If a Subject performs a decisive action without having acquired the necessary modalities (knowing, being-able), the engine flags missing competence phase.

6. **Detect missing sanction after performance** — If a decisive action occurs with no subsequent judgment, recognition, or consequence, the engine flags missing sanction.

7. **Detect false drama where no actantial position changes** — If the actantial configuration at the end of a scene is identical to the beginning, the scene contributed nothing.

8. **Detect unsupported heroicity** — A Subject achieving their Object without demonstrated qualification or meaningful opposition is structurally unsupported.

## Coherence (Greimas + Propp)
- Does every event follow causally from prior events?
- Are actantial configurations consistent through the fabula?
- Does each event serve both a Greimasian necessity and a Proppian function?

## Completeness
- Is every narrative program carried through all four canonical phases (Manipulation → Competence → Performance → Sanction)?
- Are all Proppian functions that were opened eventually closed?

## Character Consistency
- Do characters act in accordance with their established modality profiles?
- Are actantial shifts justified by changes in value-object relationship?
- Does each character have a coherent value-logic?

## No-Filler Compliance
- Does every scene pass the 5-question Greimas diagnostic?
- Does every action transform a state?
- Could any scene be removed without breaking the fabula?

## Structural Fidelity
- Does the fabula conform to the Greimasian actantial model?
- Are canonical phases in correct order?
- Are Proppian functions applied in the correct sequence and only when serving an active Greimasian program?
